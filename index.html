Tool 01

Check the published MTD income thresholds

Use whole-pound figures from your Self Assessment return—not profit and not simply the net amount a marketplace deposited. This threshold-only check stops on cases it cannot calculate; HMRC makes the real determination.

1
Which personal income sources appear on the relevant returns?

Choose all qualifying sources shown, including a ceased source when another source continues. Do not include a limited company’s turnover.

2
Have you already submitted a Self Assessment return?

HMRC says you do not need to start MTD for Income Tax until after your first return.

3
Check the limits of this small calculator

If a special case applies, the tool will stop and send you to HMRC instead of guessing.

Ignoring income being below a threshold, have you checked HMRC's other exemption rules and confirmed none applies?

These include automatic and temporary exemptions as well as digital exclusion. Check HMRC's exemption guidance ↗

Are all figures based on 12-month accounting periods?

HMRC annualises shorter or longer periods; this tool does not.

Have all sole-trade and property sources shown on those returns now ceased?

A ceased source can still count if another source continues; if every source ceased, the result changes.

Do your figures involve foreign property, an SA109 residence page, or uncertainty about your share of jointly owned property?

Residence and joint-ownership rules can change the amount HMRC assesses, so this calculator stops on those cases.

4
Enter the qualifying-income figures you know

Enter whole pounds as reported or expected on the return. Use £0 only when a selected source genuinely had no qualifying income. Unknown historical figures stop the check; the future estimate may be left entirely blank.
