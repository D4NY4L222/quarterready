What the current prototype does

The threshold checker, route planner and marketplace worksheet run entirely in JavaScript in the visitor's browser. They do not submit form data to a server, create an account, write to local storage, or intentionally retain the figures after the page is closed or refreshed.

The prototype does not load analytics, advertising scripts, tracking pixels, chat widgets or affiliate links. It does not intentionally set non-essential cookies.

The owner and data controller for QuarterReady is Danyal Ahmed. Privacy questions and correction requests can be sent to quarterready.contact@gmail.com.

What you should not enter

The tools require only summary amounts and broad workflow choices. Do not enter:

  • your name, address, email or phone number;
  • National Insurance number, UTR or Government Gateway credentials;
  • bank-account or payment-card details;
  • customer names, addresses, order IDs or messages;
  • uploaded statements, tax returns or marketplace reports.

External links

Links to GOV.UK, HMRC and any provider open an external website. Those sites have their own privacy and cookie practices. In the current prototype, provider links are ordinary untracked links.

What must change before a public launch

A public site normally produces basic hosting logs such as an IP address, request time and browser information. The final privacy notice must name the site owner/controller, hosting provider and contact route; list data categories and purposes; explain lawful bases, recipients, retention, international transfers, whether profiling occurs, individual rights, and the right to complain to the ICO.

If analytics is added, QuarterReady should prefer a privacy-minimising configuration and avoid collecting exact turnover values. “Cookieless” does not remove privacy-law duties when identifiers, hosting logs or server-side events process personal data. Non-essential storage or access must remain off before consent, with granular choices, equally easy acceptance and refusal, a later withdrawal route, and consent records.

If affiliate tracking is activated, the final notice must document each network's click identifiers, cookies or other device access; the data categories and purpose; recipients and controller roles; lawful basis; retention; international transfers; and withdrawal or objection rights. Any non-essential tracking must remain blocked until valid consent. No change should be made silently.

Launch blocker

This prototype is not ready to collect email addresses or financial files. A real data flow needs its own minimisation, security and privacy review first.
