Quick verdict

Possible fit: an Etsy seller who is publishing or revising listings and wants a repeatable way to compare keyword demand, competition, trends and listing health.

Skip or delay it: if you have not yet produced good photos, clear offers or reliable fulfilment, or if you expect a keyword tool to create demand for an unwanted product.

QuarterReady's recommendation

Use eRank's no-card free plan on three real listings before upgrading. If the research does not change a title, tag, product decision or listing test, paying for more daily lookups will not fix that.

What eRank currently provides

eRank's published plan page lists keyword research, listing audits, trend tools, rank checks, competitor monitoring and connected-shop analysis. Its free plan currently includes five keyword lookups and five listing audits per day for one shop with up to 50 active listings.

The paid tiers mainly raise limits and add more competitor, shop and listing capacity. Those limits matter to active sellers doing research regularly; they matter much less to somebody testing one or two products.

A 20-minute free-plan test

  1. Choose one listing that already has impressions but weak clicks or sales.
  2. Compare the phrase you currently target with two more specific buyer phrases.
  3. Check whether the tool reveals a meaningful difference in demand, competition or trend direction.
  4. Change one element only, record the date and leave enough time to observe Etsy's own traffic data.
  5. Upgrade only when the free daily limits repeatedly block useful work.

What the numbers cannot promise

  • eRank is independent of Etsy and cannot guarantee ranking or sales.
  • Third-party search and competitor figures are research signals, not your own shop's audited revenue.
  • Better keywords cannot rescue weak photos, pricing, product-market fit, delivery or reviews.
  • A change should be tested against Etsy's own shop data rather than credited automatically to the tool.
